Viewing Climate Change by Glacier Altitude Decline: Online Climatology Database

Developed by: Samuel Clear
Data Derived From: World Glacial Monitoring Service and the World Bank Group

As we progress into the 21st Century, our average global surface temperature has increased by 0.32 degrees Fahrenheit each decade since the 1981 (www.climate.gov). Each decade's elevating temperatures have had a devastating impact on communities all across the world, whether it be victims of Hurricane Sandy or Dorian who were impacted by the increased storm surge severities and the increased frequency of tropical storms, or the monsoon season in August leading to flooding in parts of Pakistan, to glacial landslides in Alaska and Greenland caused by global warming. Each of these hazards will become only more prevalent as the China, the United States, and countries in Europe continue to release greenhouse gases into the environment such as Methane and Carbon Dioxide, which trap more solar energy in the Earth's Atmosphere (Albedo) with each passing year.

To measure the impact of our changing climate, you as a user can compare data on changing glacier elevation, sea level, and global surface temperatures to search for relationships between different systems in the atmosphere, hydrosphere, and cryosphere of Earth. With each of these searches, you have three options: create a graph to display the data for one variable and location (Ex. Glacier Elevation in Greenland from 2014-2019), create a graph comparing two variables or locations for one variable or location (Ex. Comparing Surface Temperature Average and Rising Sea Level Differences in Iceland), or you can compare two different variables between two different locations (Ex. Glacier Elevation in Antarctica to Rising Sea Level in Australia). Each of these options can be selected from the input bars below.

However, each of these variables does not have the same amount of data. Current Glacial Density Data contains data from many countries and continents around the world such as Antarctica, Nepal, and Pakistan among others from dates between 1950-2010, but data retrieved on glacier elevation varies from 5-6 dates for one country, to thousands of data points in others. Current Average Surface Temperature Data this website contains data for the countries of Canada, Chile, Greenland, Iceland, Pakistan, and the United States of America between the years of 1901 to 2021. Each of these countries are the same for Sea Level Data, however the website database only contains data from between 1993 and 2021. However, the graphs created in the website account for this.

Please note that this database does not contain data from every country's glacial elevation, sea level, and surface temperature readings. Each of the countries chosen were selected because of their large concentration of glaciers, as well as being prevalent countries natural disasters occurring today. However, from the websites of the World Bank Group and the World Glacier Monitoring Service linked below, you can find more data from countries across the world to view how your area of study may also be affected by glacial melt or rising temperatures. Additionally, you can petition for another country by selecting the "Request a Country Addition" button below! If we receive over 25 users request for a country to be added to the database, we will release an updated version of the database and the user interface containing the new country's data that we can find!
